The Origins of Impressionism

Impressionism emerged in France during the late nineteenth century. Artists rejected strict realism and instead focused on light, movement, color, and ordinary life.

Claude Monet

Claude Monet became one of the founders of Impressionism through his exploration of changing light, atmosphere, and nature.

Vincent van Gogh

Vincent van Gogh expanded Impressionist ideas with expressive brushwork, bold colors, and emotional intensity.

Edgar Degas

Edgar Degas focused on movement, dancers, and scenes from modern Parisian life using unusual compositions and perspectives.

Pierre-Auguste Renoir

Renoir became known for warm social scenes, vibrant color, and joyful depictions of everyday life.

Mary Cassatt

Mary Cassatt portrayed intimate domestic scenes and helped bring Impressionism to audiences in the United States.
